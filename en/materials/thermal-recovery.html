Photothermal Warming Materials

Advanced surface treatments for enhanced heat retention and lightweight thermal comfort.

Material Platform Overview

HTX photothermal warming materials are designed to enhance heat retention through controlled surface-level functional treatment.

By utilizing a titanium-alloy–based surface architecture, these materials respond to light exposure with increased thermal performance while maintaining lightweight textile construction.

Photothermal Performance

Test Method: JIS L 1926 (2019)

Under controlled light exposure, Air-Coated variants demonstrate higher temperature rise compared to untreated control materials.

Photothermal Temperature Rise (ΔT)

Variant Temp Rise (ΔT)
Ti + Al (Air-Coated) 9.4°C
Ti (Air-Coated) 8.7°C
Uncoated Control 6.2°C

Functional Characteristics

  • Enhanced photothermal response under light exposure
  • Lightweight warming effect without added thickness
  • Surface-level functionality without altering base fabric structure
  • Suitable for static and low-activity thermal comfort use cases

Material Integrity

The surface treatment is designed to minimize impact on the original textile properties.

  • Color appearance remains close to the original fabric.
  • Breathability, elasticity, stretch recovery, and softness remain governed by the base fabric design.

Material Identification

HTX-22 Identification Code

Photothermal warming materials may be paired with HTX material identification upon request.

Identification tools support downstream material communication without affecting product design or branding.
