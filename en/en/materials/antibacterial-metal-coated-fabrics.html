Antibacterial Metal-Coated Fabrics

Production-ready coated textiles for close-to-skin applications — designed to reduce odor-causing bacteria while preserving comfort and wearability.

Material Overview

HTX antibacterial metal-coated fabrics are standardized material platforms engineered for hygiene-oriented textile components.

They are supplied as process-defined outputs (semi-finished or finished formats), ready for downstream cutting, lamination, sewing, or conversion.

Key Features

  • • Helps reduce odor-causing bacterial growth on textile surfaces
  • • Suitable for humid, close-to-skin environments (liners, inner layers, underwear)
  • • Designed to remain breathable and wearable for daily use
  • • Compatible with brand-side product design and styling workflows

Typical Applications

1. Underwear & Close-to-Skin Layers

Innerwear, base layers, comfort liners. Odor control and hygiene support for daily wear.

2. Footwear Linings & Insoles

Shoe lining textile, sock-liner layers, soft inserts. Hygienic support in heat/moisture heavy conditions.

3. Baby & Sensitive-Skin Products

Baby swim shorts lining / baby apparel inner layers. Emphasis on mildness and comfort.

Mildness & Wearability

Designed for close-to-skin use cases. Material selection and finishing are evaluated under partner-aligned test methods. Final product safety and claims are validated in the partner’s finished-goods context.

Material Identification

Material identification tools help partners clearly explain material-level technologies to end customers.

For materials treated with Air Coating, HTX provides optional material identification tags and info cards.

These identification tools support downstream communication from B to C, allowing brands to explain surface-level technology, functional behavior, and material value in a clear and consistent manner.

Rather than changing product appearance or branding, the hangtag serves as an informational bridge between material engineering and everyday use.
